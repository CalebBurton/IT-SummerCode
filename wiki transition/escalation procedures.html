On-Call Engineer Procedures: TSC

In the event of a service outage or other urgent issue that requires immediate attention from PIPS, TNS or ISSC, Northwestern IT staff have been instructed to call 1-HELP to get expedited assistance in reaching their on-call engineer. We should follow this procedure:
  • Users will reach us by calling 1-HELP, option 7. This routes directly to Tier 2 with the toast alert "Escalated tech support Evanston IL".
  • If user does not choose option 7 and call routes to TSC, Tier 1 should follow the steps below. Note that outside Tier 2 business hours, option 7 routes calls directly to the SOC.
    1. Make sure the user provides the following information:
      • Their name and NetID
      • Which IT department they're in
      • Their call-back number
      • Which on-call engineer role they need; if they ask for a specific engineer by name, ask what team that engineer is on
      • A brief description of the urgent issue or outage
    2. Verify all user information, especially contact information
    3. Escalate the interaction as an incident to the SO-SOC assignment group.
    4. Call the SOC at 7-2222 and ask them to page the requested engineer. If the user didn't know which engineer role they need, ask the SOC to page the PIPS-Infrastructure engineer. Give the SOC the incident number, user's name and call-back number.

Contacting an Engineer During Business Hours (8:00a – 5p M-F)

  1. Create a Service Manager Ticket and assign it to the correct on call group as determined through the process above.
  2. Call the on call Engineer
    • Identify the correct on call Engineer from the Google Calendar. Using the NUIT CyberInfrastructure Phone List, first call the cell number, then if no response, leave voice mail, and attempt the listed office number.
  3. If no response within 10 minutes:
    • Call the backup person listed on the Google Calendar. Using the NUIT CyberInfrastructure Phone List, first call the cell number, then if no response, leave vm, and attempt the listed office number.
    • You must speak with the Engineer and receive positive confirmation that he is taking ownership for the call. They will be responsible for resolving the problem.
  4. If no response within 10 minutes (20 minutes from time the report was received):
    • Call Engineers manager – located on Grid above. Using the NUIT CyberInfrastructure Phone List, call their cell number, if no response, leave a voicemail and attempt listed office number.
    • If you are unable to locate them, escalate to the Director of PIPS, Eva Basner-Katz via her cell number, located on the NUIT CyberInfrastructure Phone List.
  5. After an Engineer/Manager has been reached:
    • The SOC should contact the person initiating the incident call and let them know that the Engineer has been reached and will be contacting them for problem details. If the caller has not heard from the Engineer within 20 minutes they should contact the SOC for follow up.
    • The Engineer will contact the caller with status and set expectations for problem resolution or status update. The Engineer should also instruct the caller to contact the SOC if additional information needs to be relayed.
